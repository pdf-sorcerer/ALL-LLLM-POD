There’s honestly a novel’s worth of failures, cursed experiments, and lessons learned buried inside this pipeline. Keeping things high-level, the final architecture evolved through several major iterations:

1. I replaced Pyannote with NVIDIA NeMo. NeMo produced significantly cleaner speaker separation and tighter embeddings.
2. NeMo’s RTTM diarization boundaries warped the transcript into fragmentation patterns like:
Speaker_1: I
UNKNOWN: like
Speaker_1: tacos
3. WhisperX transcripts are word-level aligned to the millisecond, so they became the canonical source of truth rather than relying directly on NeMo RTTM boundaries. NeMo was constrained to classify speech only inside those existing transcript windows.
4. From there, I built lightweight HITL heuristics to identify suspicious transcript artifacts: ellipsis boundaries, lowercase continuations, dangling sentence terminators, speaker sandwiches, and other structural fingerprints that reliably surfaced hidden attribution failures.

Boundary Integrity Flags

AI errors at the transcription stage often leave recognizable fingerprints. Over time, a small set of heuristic flags emerged that reliably surfaced suspicious segments for Human-in-the-Loop review. See that in the next section.
