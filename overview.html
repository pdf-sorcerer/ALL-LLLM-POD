Hey, everyone! Welcome to ALL-LLM Pod: a technically serious AI system built around an intentionally unserious premise — simulating conversations with — and between — satirical versions of ALL-In Podcast personalities and other tech figures.

The personas are built from publicly available audio and video, processed into structured speaker-labeled transcripts through an AI pipeline involving speech detection, transcription, speaker attribution, and transcript repair. I have ethical concerns about the nature of this work; that’s not lost on me. But conversational AI systems built from publicly available media are arriving whether we are comfortable with them or not, and I would rather engage with that reality directly than pretend it isn’t happening.

Besides. Comedy is legal, again.

Transcripts were the most challenging, important, and rewarding part of this project. Transcripts probably feel unglamorous compared to AI coworkers, humanoid robots, and realtime movie generation. Even so, most easily-scraped Internet text has already been consumed repeatedly by modern LLMs. But increasingly, new information enters AI systems through messy realtime audio: podcasts, interviews, livestreams, debates, and conversations that first need to be transcribed, segmented, labeled, repaired, and reconstructed into reliable machine-readable text.

Transcript infrastructure is quietly becoming a critical layer that collects, cleans, and conditions new content before models can meaningfully ingest it.

That process turns out to be surprisingly fragile — and operationally important.
