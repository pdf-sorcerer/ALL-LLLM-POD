Hey, everyone! Welcome to ALL-LLM Pod: a technically serious AI system built around an intentionally unserious premise: having conversations with chatbot parodies of the ALL-In Podcast hosts.

These personalities are reconstructed from podcast episodes and other publicly available audio using a customized open-source pipeline for speech detection, transcription, speaker attribution, transcript repair, and conversational reconstruction. I have ethical grumblings about the nature of this work, and where the line is between parody and impersonation. Even so, tech leaders who broadcast not knowing what iPhone model they're using should expect some light teasing.

Transcripts ended up being the most rewarding part of the project — and easily the hardest. They're not glamorous, but they're important. The easily-scraped Internet text is already consumed by modern LLMs. New content that is factual is increasingly locked in messy human media — podcasts, interviews, livestreams, debates, conversations. That content has to be transcribed, segmented, labeled, and repaired into reliable, ground-truth, machine-readable text. Transcripts were the most rewarding part of this project (and the most challenging). Transcripts are boring for most people, unglamorous especially next to AI coworkers, package-sorting robots, and realtime Marvel-movie CGI slop. But transcripts matter much more than they look. The easily-scraped text is already baked into frontier LLMs. Going forward, real content — people sharing knowledge — is scarce, and what exists is locked in multimedia that first has to be converted, transcribed, segmented, labeled, and repaired into reliable, ground-truth text. In my experience, that process turns out to be surprisingly fragile.
