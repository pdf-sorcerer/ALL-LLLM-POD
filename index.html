Multi-Agent LLM System

ALL-LLM Pod

Click to enlarge · Live runtime and offline training pipeline

ALL-LLM Pod is a realtime multi-agent system simulating the hosts of the All-In Podcast through text messaging and video conversation modes.

The stack spans three machines over a Tailscale mesh: an iPhone client, a Windows GPU orchestration server, and Apple Silicon local inference. Users can DM personas, place FaceTime-style voice calls, and participate in group conversations between hosts.

WhisperX large-v3-turbo handles both realtime ASR and forced alignment for F5-TTS voice synthesis. Qwen3-30B-A3B runs locally on Apple Silicon with the persona prompt pinned in memory, while the orchestration layer manages conversational state, retrieval, streaming, and memory across devices.

The personas are assembled from several interacting systems: large persona prompts, a RAG corpus of 500+ speaker-labeled podcast transcripts, conversational memory, agentic memory injection, optional reasoning passes, facial recognition, and avatar face/body mapping.

The most difficult problem in the project turned out not to be inference, but transcript fidelity.

Short-form audio is commonly discarded in speech pipelines because speaker classification reliability drops sharply on brief utterances. I kept it because roughly 43% of host speech occurs in segments under five seconds. That decision forced the creation of a HITL forensic-review pipeline to detect and repair transcription and diarization failures at scale.

Late in development, another failure mode emerged: speech present in one transcript source but entirely missing from another. The issue resembled dropped audio, but behaved differently enough that I began referring to it internally as “ghost audio.” Weeks were spent tracing its causes, implications, and downstream effects on persona training quality.

The next step is building mirror episodes: a parallel version of the weekly podcast where the personas independently gather news throughout the week, discuss stories with one another asynchronously, and synthesize their own Friday episode from those conversations.
