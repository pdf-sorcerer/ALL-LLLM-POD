Multi-Agent LLM System

ALL-LLM Pod

Hey, everybody Welcome to the number one ALL-LLM Pod: a technically serious AI system built around an intentionally unserious premise: having conversations with chatbot parodies of the ALL-In Podcast hosts through text messaging and video chat.

These personalities are reconstructed from podcast episodes using an open-source CUDA pipeline for ASR and diarization Speaker audio-to-name mapping and transcript review are all human-in-the-loop.

The frontend is polished, but most of the engineering effort went into the distributed backend: local Qwen3 inference served through oMLX on Apple M4 hardware, alongside a Windows PC acting as both router and model orchestrator with a 12GB NVIDIA GPU handling CUDA workloads.

I have ethical grumblings about the nature of this work and where the line is between parody and impersonation. But I also wager that since comedy is legal again, tech leaders who publicly broadcast not knowing what model iPhone they are using should probably expect some light teasing.

This was a decent amount of work and a good self-led introduction into the world of generative AI and LLMs. You may be surprised, but the most rewarding part of the project ended up being the transcripts. They may not be glamorous, but they matter for the future of LLM development because most of the easily-scraped Internet is already baked into frontier models. New knowledge will not come as easily or as cheaply. Much of it will arrive as audio and video — podcasts, talks, interviews — which then has to be transcribed, segmented, labeled, repaired, and transformed into reliable ground-truth text. In my experience, that process is surprisingly fragile.
