01

Discovery

Building v2 transcripts of Episode 136 from a Libsyn master, I noticed Friedberg's "Oh my god" at ~21s — present in the YouTube transcript — was missing from the new one. A second "Oh my god" from Brad Gerstner at ~28s was absent from both transcripts. Acoustic verification: VAD positive on both windows, RMS energy present on both, but pitch only recoverable for Friedberg via spectral subtraction (anchored on Chamath at t=17–20). Gerstner shows energy without an extractable F0. The audio is real; something in the ASR pipeline is dropping it.

02

Decoder Probe

WhisperX runs CTranslate2 as its decoder backend, which doesn't expose internal state cleanly. I swapped to reference openai-whisper — same model weights, PyTorch decoder, fully inspectable — and looked at cross-attention across the 20–30s window, then probed token probabilities at Friedberg's anchor.

Friedberg falls into a wide attention valley (~20–22.5s). Gerstner sits in a narrow gap between two of the strongest spikes in the window. Near-zero attention at both ghosts.

Probing token probabilities directly at Friedberg's moment, on pyannote and Silero VAD chunks, crossing timestamps (TS) and condition-on-previous-tokens (COPT):

Chunk Trial TS COPT " Oh" rank " Oh" prob
pyannote v1 (production) ON TRUE 0 87.83%
pyannote v2 ON FALSE 0 34.15%
pyannote v3 OFF TRUE 0 58.93%
pyannote v4 OFF FALSE 46 0.020%
silero v1 (production) ON TRUE 14 0.226%
silero v2 ON FALSE 0 49.06%

Pyannote at production settings reaches " Oh" at 87.83% — the model almost emits the ghost on its own. Silero at the same settings collapses to 0.226% on the same audio. The chunk doesn't poison perception; it poisons the natural decode path.

Gerstner across the same probes: rank 65–134 at 0.004–0.075%. Three orders of magnitude below Friedberg. None of the trials reach his timestamp anchor — they all overshoot it via an "I'm bullying when you're--" hallucination loop terminating at <|endoftext|>.

03

Production Failure Matrix

The probe shows what the decoder sees at single positions. Production runs beam search across full sequences — different regime, different failure surface. Crossing file × engine × beam size, with the v1 YouTube transcript as control:

Audio File Engine Beam Friedberg Gerstner
YouTube (control) WhisperX (CT2) 5 ✓ recovered ✗ not found
Libsyn WhisperX (CT2) 1 ✓ recovered ✗ not found
Libsyn WhisperX (CT2) 5 ✗ not found ✗ not found
Libsyn reference Whisper (PWW) 1 ✓ recovered ✗ not found
Libsyn reference Whisper (PWW) 5 ✓ recovered ✗ not found

One failure cell for Friedberg: Libsyn × WhisperX (CT2) × beam=5 — the production-equivalent default, which is why this surfaced at all. Every other tested cell recovers him. Gerstner drops in all five.

Why beam matters. Beam=1 (greedy) commits token-by-token to the most probable next token. " Oh" is locally rank 0 at 87.83% in production state, so greedy emits the ghost. Beam=5 optimizes joint probability across the whole sequence, and on Libsyn it finds a path that skips Friedberg's quiet utterance because the longer "He's not even here..." continuation scores higher in joint probability. What's specific about the Libsyn file's audio versus YouTube's is what the audio panel investigates next.

04

Audio Modification Panel

Ten ffmpeg-filtered variants of Libsyn run through default WhisperX (CT2, beam=5) — the failure cell — to identify which audio property is the lever:

Variant Filter Recovered?
V01 baseline resample only NO
V02 gain +3 dB volume=3dB NO
V03 gain +6 dB volume=6dB NO
V04 gain −3 dB volume=-3dB NO
V05 expand mild compand (lift crest, mild) NO
V06 expand strong compand (lift crest, strong) NO
V07 compress + gain compand (lower crest) + volume=5dB YES
V08 highpass 80 Hz highpass=f=80 NO
V09 lowpass 7800 Hz lowpass=f=7800 NO
V10 loudnorm loudnorm I=-16:LRA=11:TP=-1.5 NO

One variant of ten recovered Friedberg: V07, compand compression (peaks anchored, mids pulled down, quiets pulled further down) plus a 5 dB makeup gain. Crest factor dropped from 22.97 dB to 17.90 dB. A separately-run compression test with different compand parameters also recovered him.

Rules out: gain in any direction, dynamic-range expansion (pushing crest toward YouTube's 28.1 dB made things worse), EQ filtering, loudness normalization. The earlier intuition that YouTube recovers Friedberg because of its higher dynamic range is dead — higher crest doesn't help; lower crest does. Doesn't establish: why compression specifically works. The defensible finding is that the failure is brittle to perturbation, not driven by a clean acoustic-property gradient.

Friedberg-ghost vs. Gerstner-ghost
Friedberg-ghosts are recoverable with the PWW pipeline (pyannote + reference Whisper + wav2vec). Gerstner-ghosts are unrecoverable with current tools.

PWW runs beam=5. At beam=1, the decoder falls into hallucination loops in the ghost regions — emitting "Oh, my god. Oh, my god" at Friedberg's position and "I'm bullying when you're..." near Gerstner's, the latter terminating at <|endoftext|> before the decoder ever reaches his timestamp anchor. Pyannote VAD removes silence before transcription, which should suppress Whisper's tendency to hallucinate. Instead, hallucinations appear specifically during regions of voice activity — a signal that Whisper heard something it couldn't transcribe cleanly. By happy accident, this gives a forensic probe: repetitive phrases or stuck loops in close proximity flag where additional ghosts may live.
05

The PWW Pipeline

Out: WhisperX + CTranslate2. In: pyannote VAD → reference openai-whisper (PyTorch, beam=5, temperature=0) → wav2vec2 forced alignment.

audio ↓ pyannote VAD ↓ reference openai-whisper (beam=5, temperature=0) ↓ wav2vec2 forced alignment ↓ v2 transcript (segments + word timings + per-word scores)

Cost: ~10 min/episode vs ~1–2 min for WhisperX defaults. Across 538 episodes: ~90 GPU-hours instead of ~15. Tradeoff is compute time for content fidelity. Reference Whisper picks up more hallucinations than CT2 in the process, but those hallucinations are detectable via wav2vec phonemic alignment failure, so the cost is bounded.

06

Caveats

  • Single-episode validation. Most pipeline validation lives on Episode 136. Generalization to noisier, longer, or multi-language episodes unverified.
  • Gerstner is unsolved. No tested configuration recovers him. The embedding triple-negative idea is sketched, not built.
  • Audio panel rules out, doesn't explain. Two compression-style modifications recovered Friedberg under default WhisperX; eight non-compression modifications missed. The proximate audio mechanism behind the Libsyn × beam=5 failure remains unidentified — what we have is a brittleness finding, not a causal one.
  • Heuristic thresholds. Lizard, blob, and wav2vec-score cutoffs were hand-tuned on Episode 136. Edge cases will leak.
