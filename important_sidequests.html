Important Side Quests

Many of the most interesting engineering problems in ALL-LLM POD were not directly related to the chatbot itself. These side projects emerged while solving practical problems around transcription, speaker attribution, retrieval, dataset quality, and operational scale.

Together, they form much of the infrastructure that makes the main application possible.

Clownfish Remote operations console for corpus maintenance. Ingestion · orchestration · corpus ops +

Named after Nvidia NeMo's phenomenal speaker embeddings, Clownfish was created as a human-in-the-loop labeling tool that combines transcripts, audio snippets, and NeMo diarization output to map real-world identities to diarized speaker IDs.

These mappings transform anonymous speaker labels into known participants across the corpus. The same workflow also enables large-scale extraction of speaker-specific audio, creating clean datasets for downstream applications such as voice cloning, speaker classification, and persona generation.

As of June 2026, Clownfish's latest evolution is its transformation into a remote operations layer for the corpus, capable of discovering and queueing new content, orchestrating transcription and diarization pipelines, and supporting downstream human-in-the-loop review workflows from any device, including PC, Mac, iPhone, and browser.

Transcript Forensics & Repair Brody: human-in-the-loop transcript quality control. Transcript QA · lexical repair · HITL +

Automated pipelines produce remarkably good transcripts—until they don't. Brody provides forensic tooling for identifying attribution errors, transcript corruption, segmentation mistakes, and other edge cases that can silently degrade downstream datasets.

Ghost Audio When the audio exists, but the transcript doesn't. ASR failure modes · missing speech · transcript reliability +

During transcript validation, I discovered multiple cases where audible speech appeared in source media but was missing from generated transcripts. This investigation became a deeper exploration of ASR failure modes, transcript reliability, and the challenges of building trustworthy datasets.

Memory & Retrieval Mining years of conversation for recurring arguments. Argument mining · retrieval · persona memory +

Transcripts are excellent for speaker mimicry, but I wanted to mine them further for argument structures, recurring positions, and episode-level narrative arcs. The long-term goal was to generate "Black Mirror" episodes: synthetic conversations grounded in the hosts' historical reasoning patterns rather than simply their speaking style.
